selected publications

  1. Under Review
    Unison: Harmonizing Motion, Speech, and Sound for Human-Centric Audio-Video Generation
    Shihao Cheng, Jiaxu Zhang, Quanyue Song, and 6 more authors
    First Author
    Abs arXiv Bib

    We propose Unison, a unified framework for human-centric audio-video generation that harmonizes motion, speech, and ambient sound. Unison resolves the speech-SFX interference and motion-audio desynchronization problems that have long limited audio-video generative models, and produces temporally aligned, semantically consistent multi-modal outputs.

  2. Under Review
    InteractiveAvatar: Real-Time Streaming Video Generation for Consistent and Intent-Aware Avatars
    Shihao Cheng and others
    Abs

    InteractiveAvatar tackles the understanding-interaction gap and the long-term semantic drift in infinite-length video generation. We design a long-short term token memory mechanism that lets the avatar follow user intent and remain visually consistent across arbitrarily long streams.

  3. CVPR 2026
    GeoMMBench and GeoMMAgent: Toward Expert-Level Multimodal Intelligence in Geoscience and Remote Sensing
    Aoran Xiao, Shihao Cheng, Yonghao Xu, and 3 more authors
    Co-First Author
    In Proceedings of the IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2026
    CVPR Highlight Abs arXiv Bib

    Highlight Paper at CVPR 2026

    We introduce GeoMMBench, the first expert-level multimodal evaluation benchmark for interdisciplinary, multi-sensor geoscience and remote-sensing scenarios, together with GeoMMAgent, a multi-agent architecture that achieves strong performance on the benchmark.

  4. T-CSVT 2025
    OwlSight: A Robust Illumination Adaptation Framework for Dark Video Human Action Recognition
    Shihao Cheng, Jinlu Zhang, Yue Liu, and 2 more authors
    First Author
    IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology, 2025
    Abs HTML Bib

    OwlSight resolves the inefficient brightness utilization that bottlenecks low-light enhancement and action recognition end-to-end training. The framework jointly optimizes illumination adaptation and action recognition for robust dark-video understanding.
